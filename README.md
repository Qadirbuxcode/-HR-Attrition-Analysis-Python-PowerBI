# -HR-Attrition-Analysis-Python-PowerBI
This project analyzes employee data from a company with 50 employees to find out why people are leaving (called attrition). Using Python for data analysis and chart creation, and Power BI for the final dashboard, this project gives the HR team clear answers about which departments have problems, which employees are most at risk, and what the company should do to fix retention.

# Business Problem
The HR manager noticed that several employees had resigned in the past year but did not know the main reasons. Were people leaving because of low salary? Too much overtime? No career growth? This analysis answers those questions using real employee data

# Key Results Found
•	Overall attrition rate: 36% — meaning 18 out of 50 employees left
•	Sales department: 60% attrition rate — the worst department by far
•	Average salary of employees who left: PKR 42,000 (vs PKR 71,000 for those who stayed)
•	80% of employees who left were doing overtime
•	70% of employees who left were aged between 23 and 30
•	Employees who travel frequently for business left at 65% rate

# Charts Generated by Python
•	Bar Chart — Attrition rate by department (red bar = danger zone)
•	Histogram — Salary comparison: employees who left vs stayed
•	Bar Chart — Number of employees who left by age group
•	Grouped Bar Chart — Overtime vs attrition
•	Grouped Bar Chart — Job satisfaction score vs attrition

# HR Recommendations Included
•	Increase base salary for Sales department — currently PKR 42,000 average which is too low
•	Limit overtime to maximum 5 hours per week for all departments
•	Add a mentoring and career growth plan for employees aged under 30
•	Rotate travel duties more fairly across the team

# Tools & Skills Demonstrated
•	Python: Pandas (data cleaning, groupby), Matplotlib & Seaborn (5 different chart types), openpyxl (Excel export)
•	Excel: Formatted tables, pivot tables, conditional formatting, department summary
•	Power BI: Attrition KPI cards, bar chart, donut chart, slicers by department and gender
